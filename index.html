Present Simple

Use it. Understand it. Master it.

1. What is it?

The Present Simple is used to talk about actions that are regular, true, or always happen.

Keywords:

always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day, on Mondays.

2. Uses
🪥

Habits

I brush my teeth every morning.

🌍

Truths

Water boils at 100°C.

📖

Facts

Bananas grow in tropical countries.

🚌

Schedules

The bus leaves at 8 o’clock.

❤️

Feelings

I love chocolate.

3. Form
Subject Affirmative Negative Question
I / You / We / They I play football. I don’t play football. Do I play football?
He / She / It He plays football. He doesn’t play football. Does he play football?
4. Spelling Rules
  • Most verbs → add -s: play → plays
  • Verbs ending in -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o → add -es: watch → watches
  • Consonant + y → change y to i + es: study → studies
  • Vowel + y → add -s: enjoy → enjoys
5. Adverbs of Frequency
100% always
80% usually
60% often
40% sometimes
20% rarely
0% never

They usually come before the main verb. Example: I always wake up early.

6. Time Expressions
  • every day / week / month / year
  • once a week / twice a month
  • on Mondays
  • in the morning / evening
  • at night / at the weekend
7. Common Mistakes
  • ❌ He go to school. → ✅ He goes to school.
  • ❌ She don’t like coffee. → ✅ She doesn’t like coffee.
  • ❌ Do they goes? → ✅ Do they go?
